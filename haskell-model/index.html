EV Forecasting Data in India

Executive Summary: This project uses a Haskell-based forecasting engine to predict the 2040 Indian EV roadmap. By using 15 years of Vahan Registry Data, we prove that India's transition is driven by infrastructure reality and fleet adoption (Ola/Uber) rather than just sales hype.

Project Technical FAQs

Models Which models were used and what factors are they based on? +
We used four mathematical models, each focusing on a different factor:
1. Logistic Model: Based on Carrying Capacity ($K$). It calculates the maximum limit of cars on Indian roads.
2. Gompertz Model: Based on Asymmetry. It handles the "Delayed Start" factor where adoption is slow at the beginning.
3. Bass Diffusion: Based on Social Influence. It splits factors into $p$ (Innovators/Tech-lovers) and $q$ (Imitators/Common People).
4. ARIMAX: Based on Economic Variables. It adjusts the growth based on external factors like GDP and Oil prices.
Data What specific factors were taken in our forecasting engine? +
Our Haskell engine considers 5 "Real World" factors to calculate the 2040 roadmap:
1. Battery Prices ($/kWh): The primary cost driver. Sales rise as prices fall below $100/kWh.
2. Fuel Costs (Petrol/Diesel): The higher the petrol price, the faster the switch to EVs.
3. Charging Station Density: A "Hard Cap" factor—sales cannot cross grid capacity.
4. Consumer Spending (GDP): Reflects the ability of the middle class to buy new cars.
5. The Neighbor Effect: Word-of-mouth influence among Indian families.
Comparison Why is our 2040 forecast lower than Bloomberg? +
Bloomberg assumes people will buy cars regardless of chargers. We used "Grid-Constraint Logic." India's wires cannot support 60 lakh cars at once yet. Our 20 lakh predicted number is honest and safer for planning.
Timeline Which model was used for which years? +
1. 2010-2024: Vahan Actuals.
2. 2025-2030: Bass + Gompertz (Captures the "Rocket" boom).
3. 2031-2040: Logistic + ARIMAX (Captures market saturation).
PFL Why use Haskell vs other languages like Python? +
As a PFL researcher, I chose Haskell for Immutability. In Python, variables change randomly (Side-Effects), which causes math errors. Haskell ensures our growth curves for 2040 are pure and error-free.
Concepts Which PFL concepts were used? +
1. Pure Functions: For math curves.
2. Higher-Order Functions: Merging models with zipWith.
3. Recursion: For calculating year-on-year ARIMAX growth.
